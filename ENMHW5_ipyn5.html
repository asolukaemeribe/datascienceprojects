Unicorn Starups and Venture Captial¶

Question¶

I am a venture capitalist looking to make some investments in some untapped emerging markets and hence need you to provide me insights. Provide me a list of 5 companies to spend my money on, based on the following criteria: (1) reached unicorn status recently (2) operate in a country with few investments (3) operate in an niche but still profitable sector.

Abstract¶

I am a data scientist and my customer is a venture capitalist looking for a solution to handle the startup seeding market. My customer wants a definitive list of well established but niche startups to invest in. We can infer the goal of our stakeholder is to make as much money as possible by becoming an investor for a startup with history of fast growth and slim competition in its respective sector. Therefore, this project will be defining the metrics from data we must use to help formulate the list our stakeholder wants and some statistical methods to be used will include clustering and regression analysis among others. Additionally, this study and findings would be useful to those in venture capital with similar goals.

Answering Initial Question¶

Metric Definiton

Startup Ranking Metric = ((valuation) x (popularity ranking of startup's industry)) / ((number of unique investors in city startup is based in) x (days since achieved unicorn status)) = SRM

Normalized SRM = (SRM - min_SRM) / (max_SRM - min_SRM)

Startup Ranking Metric (SRM) Calculation

Conclusion¶

The top five startup companies that our data searching algorithm found based on our stakeholder's criteria were Rimac Automobili, Getir, GoStudent, Bolt, and J&T Express. Each company is fairly new to the unicorn startup group (oldest joined in 2018) and is based in a European/Asian country with little investor prescence and niche industries; all are highly valued in respect to average valuation for the dataset. From here, our stakeholder may choose accordingly where their investments may go with further context about each company not represneted in our data.

We know this method works since the metric expression has four independent variables that all weigh the exact same, as implied by the venture capatalist's question. Additionally, the higher the valuation and/or the higher down the ranking list a startup's industry is, the raw value of our metric will increase; the lower the number of investors there are and/or the lower the number of days it's been since the startup's unicorn status was established, the raw value of our metric will also increase, the inverse is true for the opposite directions. We normalize these metric values to control for varying scales in raw value. This method of quanitifying criteria to operational define a metric is credible and can be easily modified in the case there was a stakeholder with different needs. The signficance of this project is that now, the venture capitalist who asked the intial question now has a definitive list of companies given their criteria with high investemnt potential to invest money into.
